about me

Sheldon Lee Cooper, Ph.D., Sc.D., is a fictional character in the CBS television series The Big Bang Theory
and its spinoff series Young Sheldon, portrayed by actors Jim Parsons and Iain Armitage respectively
(with Parsons as the latter series' narrator). For his portrayal, Parsons won four Primetime Emmy Awards,
a Golden Globe Award, a TCA Award, and two Critics' Choice Television Awards. The character's childhood is
the focus of Young Sheldon: the series' first season is set in 1989 when nine-year-old prodigy Sheldon has
skipped ahead five grades, to start high school alongside his older brother.

The adult Sheldon is a senior theoretical physicist at The California Institute of Technology (Caltech),
and for the first ten seasons of The Big Bang Theory shares an apartment with his colleague and best
friend, Leonard Hofstadter (Johnny Galecki); they are also friends and coworkers with Howard Wolowitz
(Simon Helberg) and Rajesh Koothrappali (Kunal Nayyar). In season 10, Sheldon moves across the hall with his
girlfriend Amy Farrah Fowler (Mayim Bialik), in the former apartment of Leonard's wife Penny (Kaley Cuoco)

When we want to search on the Internet for example "side effects of drugs"
In WEB1.0 we will receive read-only results, on a static and non-personalized website. in basic design
In WEB2.0 we will get results for sites that support reading and writing. That is, a dynamic site
to which we can also add information, or see information that other people have added.
But even here the results will not be personalized. The website design will be more advanced and dynamic
In WEB3.0 we will receive results for sites that support reading and writing, as well as customization.
That is, we will get a dynamic website that will return us results tailored specifically to us. The sites use
advanced artificial intelligence tools to adjust personal results.
The design of the website will be advanced and adapted
In summary web1 will return static information, WEB2 dynamic information, WEB3 custom information
If we want to search for example the question "what is physics"
web 1 - we will enter a permanent website that will show us an answer to the question.
web 2 - we will visit a dynamic website where we can see other people's answers, respond to others'
answers and even add a better answer ourselves
web 3 - We will write all the information we want to know about physics, and the artificial intelligence combined
with our search history will know how to place the most adapted results for us
